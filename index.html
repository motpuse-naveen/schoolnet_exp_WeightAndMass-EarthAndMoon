Instructions

1.

Introduction to Current Ratings

Every electrical device has a current rating - it specifies the maximum current that can flow through it.

Current greater than the maximum permitted current, can cause the device to change its behaviour, and offen break the device.

The ammeter reading is noted (say 0.5 Amps)

4.

To find out the current passing through a wire, remove the wire and replace it with an Ammeter. Note the reading on the Ammeter. That is the current passing through the wire.

What is the current passing through F and G, (the bulb)?

We cannot remove the bulb and replace it with an Ammeter. That itself will change the circuit and the current.

6.

We have to recognize that the current that flows through EF is the same current that flows through FG (bulb) and through GH.

So we replace either EF or GH with the Ammeter, and take the reading.

7.

An Ammeter is like a fully conducting wire, except that it also measures the current flowing through it. Place it in a circuit where a wire should be.
