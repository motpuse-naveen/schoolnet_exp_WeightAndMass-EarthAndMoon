Instructions

1.

Weight and Mass on Earth and Moon

This exploriment shows how the difference in gravitational force on Earth and Moon affects weight and mass of the given bodies.

2.

The screen is divided into two parts:

The left part is Earth Surface The right part is Moon Surface

The device, (in this case a bulb) is placed in series with an ammeter and the two are attached to a battery with a voltage of 1 V

The ammeter reading is noted (say 0.5 Amps)

3.

You are given —

a) An Electronic Weighing Scale

b) A Spring Balance

Both instruments measure the force of gravity acting on a body, i.e. WEIGHT

4.

You are also provided with a Pan Balance

Since it has two separate pans, and both the pans are equally affected by gravity, it shows the same measurement immaterial of the magnitude of the gravitational force.

Therefore, a pan-balance measures the MASS of a body, which does not change from place to place.

5.

You are also given some colored balls and some weights to conduct your observations.

6.

To begin with, explore the two environments.

On Earth, drag and drop the ball from a height and observe how it falls. Next drag it to the right pane (moon) and see how it falls.

On Moon, it falls substantially slower as the gravitational force is only 1/6th that on Earth.

7.

Now take any object and use the electronic weighing machine and spring balance to weigh it.

Drag the same instrument(s) over to the right pane to weigh on the Moon.

You will find that all objects weigh 1/6th that on Earth.

8.

You should also use the pan balance to measure the mass of the ball on Earth and Moon.

Even though the gravitational force is reduced on the Moon, it is reduced equally for BOTH pans.

The balance COMPARES weight with a fixed standard. Therefore, you will find that the mass measurement DOES NOT VARY between Earth and Moon.

9.

The electronic balance and spring balance measure WEIGHT. Weight is the extent of gravitational force on a body. It changes from place to place.

10.

The gravitational acceleration on the surface of Earth is approximately 9.8 m/s2.

Hence, a body of mass 1 kg will weigh:
1kg x 9.8 m/s2 = 9.8 N(newtons)

Since weight is a type of force, it is measured in newton (N) and not kilogram.

11.

The pan balance measures mass. Mass is an intriinsic property of a body and is the extent of bulk of the body. It is FIXED.

Mass is a scalar quantity and is measured in kilogram (kg).
